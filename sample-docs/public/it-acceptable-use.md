# IT Acceptable Use Policy

This policy describes the acceptable use of company computing resources, including laptops, mobile devices, cloud accounts, and the corporate network. It applies to all employees, contractors, and interns who hold an active company account.

## Devices and accounts

Company-issued devices are intended primarily for work use. Limited personal use is allowed provided it does not interfere with job duties, consume excessive bandwidth or storage, or expose the company to legal or security risk. Devices must remain enrolled in the corporate device management profile at all times.

Company accounts must use the configured single sign-on identity provider. Local accounts on company-managed laptops are permitted only for IT support purposes and must be disabled when not in active use.

## Passwords and authentication

All employees must use the password manager provisioned by IT for storing work credentials. Multi-factor authentication is required on every account that supports it. Sharing of credentials between people is prohibited. If a credential must be shared with a service or automation, it must be stored in the secrets manager with appropriate access controls.

## Network use

Use of the corporate VPN is required when accessing internal applications from outside the office. Public Wi-Fi may be used with the VPN active. Personal hotspots are acceptable for short-term use during travel.

The corporate network is monitored for security threats. Employees should expect that traffic may be logged and inspected for security purposes consistent with applicable law and the company privacy policy.

## Data handling

Confidential information may not be stored on personal devices, personal cloud accounts, or unmanaged removable media. When sharing confidential information with external parties, employees must use the approved file sharing platform with link expiry and recipient restrictions enabled.

If a device is lost or stolen, report it to `it-security@example.com` immediately so the device can be remotely wiped.

## Reporting concerns

Suspected policy violations or security incidents should be reported to `it-security@example.com` or the anonymous ethics hotline. Retaliation against good-faith reporters is prohibited.
