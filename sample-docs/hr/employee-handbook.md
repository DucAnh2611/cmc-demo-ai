# Employee Handbook 2026

This handbook covers paid time off, remote work expectations, code of conduct, and onboarding for all employees of the company starting fiscal year 2026. It supersedes any prior handbook version.

## Paid time off

Full-time employees accrue twenty days of paid time off per calendar year. Time off accrues monthly at a rate of 1.66 days. Up to five unused days may be carried over into the next calendar year; anything above five is forfeited unless local labor law requires otherwise.

Sick leave is separate from PTO and is uncapped subject to manager approval. Medical certification is required for absences longer than three consecutive working days.

## Remote work

The default working arrangement is hybrid: three days in the office, two days remote, on a schedule agreed with the team. Fully remote arrangements are evaluated on a case-by-case basis and must be approved by the department head and HR.

Employees working remotely are expected to maintain a quiet workspace, a stable internet connection, and core hours overlap of at least four hours with their team's primary timezone.

## Code of conduct

All employees must adhere to the company code of conduct, which prohibits harassment, discrimination, retaliation, and the misuse of company information or assets. Reports may be made to a manager, HR, or the anonymous ethics hotline at `ethics@example.com`.

Conflicts of interest must be disclosed to the People Operations team within seven days of becoming aware of them. Failure to disclose may result in disciplinary action.

## Onboarding

New hires complete onboarding over their first two weeks. The plan covers IT setup, role-specific training, a meet-and-greet schedule, and two thirty-minute check-ins with their manager and HR business partner. A formal probation review takes place at the end of the third month.

For any handbook questions, contact `peopleops@example.com`.
