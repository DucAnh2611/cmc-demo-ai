# Q3 Compensation Policy

This document describes the compensation framework for Q3 of fiscal year 2026, including base salary bands, performance bonus structure, and the quarterly review cycle. It is owned by the People Operations team and reviewed by Finance and Legal each quarter.

## Salary bands

Base salary is determined by job family and level. Each level has a 25th, 50th, and 75th percentile pegged to market data refreshed every six months. Managers may not set offers outside the band without approval from the VP of People and the CFO.

- IC1 → IC4: individual contributor track, 4 levels
- M1 → M3: people-management track, 3 levels
- E1 → E3: executive track, 3 levels

Cross-level moves require a calibration review. Geographic differentials are applied via a location factor of 0.8 to 1.2 relative to the headquarters benchmark.

## Performance bonus

Performance bonuses are paid quarterly. The target is 10 percent of base for IC roles, 15 percent for managers, and 25 percent for executives. Payout multiplier ranges from 0.0 (does not meet) to 1.5 (significantly exceeds), capped at 1.5 even for outlier performers. The multiplier is set in the calibration meeting at the end of each quarter.

## Review cycle

The performance review cycle runs on the calendar quarter. Each cycle has four phases: self review, manager review, calibration, and outcome communication. Calibration is run at the department level by the relevant VP, with HR business partners facilitating. Outcomes are communicated within seven business days of calibration close.

## Eligibility and exceptions

Employees on a Performance Improvement Plan are not eligible for bonus payout during the active PIP window. New hires who joined after the calibration cutoff date receive a prorated payout in the following cycle.

For any exceptions, contact `compensation@example.com`. All exception decisions must be logged in the People Ops case tracker and signed off by the VP of People.
