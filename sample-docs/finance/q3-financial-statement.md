# Q3 Financial Statement (Internal)

This is the internal-only version of the Q3 financial summary, prepared by the Finance team and reviewed by the CFO. Public investor-facing numbers are released separately.

## Revenue

Total revenue for Q3 reached one hundred fourteen million dollars, up twelve percent year over year. The growth came primarily from the enterprise segment, which contributed seventy-eight million, with mid-market and small business contributing twenty-four million and twelve million respectively.

Recurring revenue accounted for ninety-one percent of the total, reflecting continued progress on the multi-year shift toward subscription pricing. Average contract value for new logos increased eight percent quarter over quarter.

## Costs

Cost of goods sold totaled thirty-six million, yielding a gross margin of sixty-eight percent. Operating expenses came in at fifty-two million, with the largest items being headcount thirty-one million, infrastructure nine million, and sales and marketing ten million.

Headcount expense was three percent under plan due to delayed senior hires, partially offset by higher contractor spend for the data platform migration.

## EBITDA and cash

EBITDA for the quarter was twenty-six million, a margin of twenty-three percent. Cash and equivalents at quarter-end stood at one hundred ninety-two million, with no draw on the revolver. Days sales outstanding was forty-one days, in line with the prior quarter.

## Outlook

For Q4, finance is guiding revenue of one hundred twenty-one to one hundred twenty-five million and EBITDA margin of twenty-two to twenty-five percent. The main risks are extended enterprise sales cycles in EMEA and a potential shift in cloud unit pricing.
