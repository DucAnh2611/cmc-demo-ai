# Vendor Payment Process

This document describes the end-to-end process for paying vendors, including the approval chain, supporting documentation, and timing. It applies to all corporate purchases above five hundred dollars.

## Onboarding a new vendor

Before a vendor can be paid, they must be onboarded in the procurement system. The requesting employee submits a vendor onboarding request including a W-9 or local tax form, banking instructions on company letterhead, and a recent certificate of insurance for service providers. Procurement performs a sanctions and watchlist screen and a duplicate check.

The onboarding turnaround target is five business days. Expedited onboarding is possible for urgent operational needs and requires sign-off from the department VP.

## Purchase orders

All spend above five hundred dollars requires a purchase order issued before any work or shipment begins. The PO captures the agreed scope, amount, payment terms, and expected delivery date. POs are reviewed by Finance and, for amounts above twenty-five thousand dollars, by the CFO.

Change orders use the same approval thresholds as the original PO. Splitting a purchase to avoid an approval threshold is prohibited and is grounds for disciplinary action.

## Invoice approval

Vendors submit invoices to `ap@example.com`. Accounts Payable matches each invoice against an active PO and the corresponding goods receipt. Three-way match must succeed before the invoice is posted to the ledger.

The first level of approval is the budget owner, typically a director. Invoices above ten thousand dollars require a second approval from the department VP. Invoices above one hundred thousand dollars require CFO approval.

## Payment terms and timing

Standard payment terms are net thirty from the invoice date. Early payment discounts are accepted only when the effective annualized return exceeds the company's cost of capital. Wire transfers are the default payment method for international vendors; domestic vendors are paid via ACH.

For exceptions or escalations, contact `ap@example.com` with the PO number and invoice number.
